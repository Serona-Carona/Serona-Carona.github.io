Incubation Period

The incubation period is the observation period from virus exposure to possible onset. According to official information from the World Health Organization and mainland China, the incubation period for new coronavirus infection in 2019 is 2 to 12 days (average 7 days). But China reports some cases had incubation period up to 37 days. Contact with patients suspected of having a new coronavirus infection in 2019 still requires 14 days of health surveillance.

The incubation period is as long as 14 days. Even infected persons with no fever, no signs of infection or only mild signs of infection can pass the virus on to others, and symptom screening cannot be effectively detected. This means that it is more difficult to control than an outbreak of the Middle East Respiratory Syndrome (MERS) or Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome (SARS).

Bruce Aylward, assistant director-general of the World Health Organization, said that Radixivir is currently the only drug that "considered to have real efficacy". The American Institute of Allergy and Infectious Diseases has begun clinical trials of the drug. There is no vaccine for COVID-19 yet.
